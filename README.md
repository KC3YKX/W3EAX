## About

The **Amateur Radio Association at the University of Maryland** holds the callsign **W3EAX** and has been connecting Terps to the world since 1933. Founded by students who borrowed equipment from the Physics Department and strung an antenna between Morrill Hall and a nearby water tower, the club made its first official contact — with an operator in Washington, D.C. — on **January 5, 1934**.

Over nine decades later, W3EAX continues to provide UMD students and staff with opportunities to learn about and participate in amateur radio communications. Our station antenna sits atop the **South Campus Dining Hall**, where the club meets and operates today.

No license? No problem. Members range from seasoned Extra-class operators to complete beginners. If you're interested in radio, you belong here.

## History & Legacy

W3EAX has a remarkable track record that goes well beyond hobby radio:

- **Three Mile Island (1979)** — When the TMI nuclear plant suffered a meltdown, W3EAX operators coordinated with hams near Harrisburg to relay real-time information to news stations across Maryland, hours before the first official press conference. Local station WLMD publicly credited W3EAX for their critical role during the crisis.

- **SPRE / STS-72 (1996)** — Club members collaborated with NASA Goddard Space Flight Center on the **Spartan Packet Radio Experiment (SPRE)**, a shuttle-based amateur radio experiment that flew aboard STS-72 in January 1996.

- **QSL Card Collection** — Over the decades, W3EAX has accumulated hundreds of QSL cards from contacts around the globe — from Kiribati to Europe — many featuring original artwork and cultural illustrations.

- **Field Day & Public Service** — The club has a long tradition of participating in ARRL Field Day exercises and emergency communication preparedness.

## What We Do

🔭 **On-Air Operations** — HF, VHF, and UHF operation from our campus station  
🎓 **License Prep** — Study sessions and practice exams for Technician, General, and Extra  
🛰️ **Experimentation** — Digital modes, satellite contacts, APRS, SDR, and more  
📡 **Contesting** — Participation in ARRL contests and special event stations  
🤝 **Elmering** — Experienced members mentor newcomers in all aspects of ham radio  
🎈 **Projects** — High-altitude balloons, fox hunts, antenna building, and homebrew electronics
